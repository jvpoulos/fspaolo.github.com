k-means clustering

k-means clustering is a method for finding clusters and cluster centers in unlabeled data. The goal is to choose k cluster centers to minimize the total squared distance between each data point and its closest center. Given randomly chosen k initial centers, the k-means algorithm alternates between two steps until convergence: (assignment step) for each center, find subset of points or "cluster" that is closer to any other center; (update step) for each cluster, compute vector containing means of each feature, which becomes new cluster center.
