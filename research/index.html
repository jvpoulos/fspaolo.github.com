Wealth, Officeholding, and Legislative Ideology

[paper] [data repository] [pre-analysis plan]

Does personal wealth translate into political power, and does it influence legislators' ideology? This study investigates the role of personal wealth in politics using the 1805 Georgia land lottery as a natural experiment. The majority of adult white males living in Georgia participated and about 15% of participants won a land prize worth over half of median wealth. I estimate the effect of winning a lottery prize on ex--post officeholding and on votes in favor of slavery legislation for participants who served in Georgia's legislature. I find no evidence that wealth affects officeholding or legislators' support for slavery. I use property tax records to show that the treatment effect on support for slavery varies systematically according legislators' pretreatment wealth.
